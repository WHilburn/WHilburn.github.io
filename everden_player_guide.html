Welcome to

Everden

Long ago, the world belonged to the Smooth Ones. When they vanished, the Great Awakening began. Once-mindless animals stood upright, spoke their first words, and inherited everything. This is the world they built.

The Great Powers
Goldcollar
Where you begin
The Heart of Everden

A sophisticated union of cats and dogs, though home to many folk. The masters of trade, luxury, and diplomacy, Goldcollar is the closest thing Everden has to a cosmopolitan heart. Its capital, Petsburg, is a waterfront city where fortunes are made and lost over a single evening's dinner.

Elegance Commerce Social Standing

Home to several prestigious mage colleges and a thriving market for enchanted goods. Artificers sell their wares openly in Petsburg, and magic items scavenged from ruins fetch high prices at auction. The surrounding countryside hides old Smooth One structures beneath overgrown hills, drawing adventurers and treasure hunters, as well as bandits and plunderers.

Leaders: Queen Daisy (Golden Retriever lineage) and King Wrinkles (Sphinx Cat), the Dual Monarchs
Carrovia
The Land of Eternal Autumn
The East

Once known for it's swift knights and beautiful meadows, this kingdom of rabbits and ravens has grown cold and insular in recent years. A thick mist now hugs it's borders, and travelers who cross into Carrovia rarely speak warmly of the experience.

Tradition Insular Martial Pride

The mists at Carrovia's borders are not natural, and locals whisper that stranger things walk the autumn woods after dark. Undead have been sighted near old ruins in the eastern reaches, and the Count's court has grown quiet on the subject. Those who investigate rarely file a complete report.

Leader: Count Barnaby von Hop
Whiskerburrow
The Home of Scholars
The Heartland

A cozy, communal nation of mice, otters, and badgers. Whiskerburrow is home to the finest academic minds in Everden and it's most dependable farmers. The people here act slowly, but are quicker to think than most. Whiscarians will happily argue about the true nature of the Great Awakening over three cups of tea.

Academics Storytelling Community

Whiskerburrow's college of natural philosophy is the oldest academic institution in Everden, producing more druids and wizards per capita than any other nation. Magic here tends toward the scholarly and the careful. The surrounding farmland is dotted with low, ancient ruins that scholars have catalogued but not fully explored.

Governance: A council of democraticly elected Elders
Iron-Tusk
The Industrial Power
The Mountains

A mountainous industrial nation ruled by boars and horses. If something in Everden is made of steel, there is a good chance it came from an Iron-Tusk forge. The people here do not waste words, and they are suspicious of anyone who does. Their cities are built to last a thousand years. They were only a few years ago in vicious war with the nation of Moonhowl.

Strength Industry Craftsmanship

Magic in Iron-Tusk is practical above all else: artificers are employed directly by the Forge Council, and enchanted tools are standard issue in the deepest mines. Those mines occasionally break through into something older than the Smooth Ones, and what lives in those deeper places is not well understood.

Governance: The Forge Council, headed by the Grand Tusk
Sunreach
The Ancient Jungle
The South

A region that's home to many reptile and amphibian-kin who guard and worship the ruins of the Smooth Ones, and a large variety of biomes including deserts, swamps, and rainforests. Outsiders are tolerated, but welcomed is a different question. It's people are slow to trust and impossible to rush. The jungle and desert swallow adventurers regularly. Here, Dragons, of which two are known, are worshiped openly. Yddross, dragon of the rainforest, and Egyyt, dragon of the desert. They don't seem hostile, but nobody speaks their language.

Stoicism Tradition Sun-Worship

Sunreach holds the largest concentration of Smooth One ruins on the continent, and it's mage college, the Otto Tomize Academy of Thaumaturgy, teaches magic drawn directly from studying them.

Governance: The Sun Council, a body of ancient ritual keepers
Moonhowl
The Frozen North
The Frontier

A wild, nomadic territory of wolves, foxes, and bears who patrol the frigid edge of the civilized world. Here, might is right, and only the strong survive. Leadership is decentralized here, and cities fend for themselves, only working together in extraordinary circumstances. Moonhowl is renowned for it's hunters, marksman, and gladiators. The nation banded together to wage with Iron-Tusk not that many years ago.

Survival Freedom Instinct

Magic in Moonhowl is shamanic and instinctive, passed down through oral tradition rather than college study. The frozen north is home to enormous creatures that would qualify as monsters anywhere else, and big game hunters make their reputations tracking them. Strange lights have been seen above the northern glaciers that no one has been able to explain.

Governance: Pack Alphas; no central authority
A Note on the Silent

Not every animal woke up. While you stand upright and wear clothes, many of your cousins remained the Silent: non-sentient beasts of the wild. Hunting Silent animals for food is a reality of life in Everden, but it is a grim one. Most folk would never eat a Silent animal of a similar species to their own, and the line between Awakened and Silent is a subject of genuine philosophical anxiety in every nation.

Other nations of Everden

You may wish to create a character that does not fit neatly into the listed playable races or list of nations, but Everden is an expansive world, and there are plenty of nations and animalfolk outside of what I've listed. I encourage you to work with me to make your idea a reality!

Magic in Everden

Magic exists in Everden, though it is not so common that anyone takes it for granted. Mage colleges operate in Goldcollar, Sunreach, and Whiskerburrow. Sorcerers with magic bloodlines appear most often in noble families, though they are not unheard of among commoners. Artificers sell enchanted goods in Petsburg and a handful of other major cities, but many of the most interesting magic items in the world were pulled out of Smooth One ruins by people who were probably not supposed to be there. Anyone with magical ability or skill in alchemy will never struggle to find work, whether in a royal court or running their own business. Player characters, naturally, tend to find adventure more compelling than either.

Monsters and Dangers

Everden is not a safe world. The Silent are not the only threat in the wilderness: creatures that would be at home in any dungeon lurk in the ruins of the Smooth Ones, in deep caves, in old forests, and in places that have not seen light in a very long time. Most populated areas are reasonably secure, but the ruins are another matter entirely. Dragons exist. They are rare, largely confined to the deep wilderness and the interior of Sunreach, and the subject of significant academic disagreement about their origins. Whether they predate the Smooth Ones or are somehow their legacy is a question nobody has managed to ask one directly and survive to report on.

The Dawnthumper
He Who Greets the Morning
▾

Depicted as a golden rabbit standing upright on a hilltop, ears raised toward a sunrise, the Dawnthumper is the patron of new beginnings, spring harvests, and swift legs. He is said to have thumped the ground three times at the moment of the Great Awakening, sending the tremor of consciousness through every burrow and warren in the land. His temples face east, and services are held at dawn, often beginning before the sun has fully crested the horizon.

The horsefolk of Iron-Tusk have begun to adopt their own vision of this god, calling him the Dawntrotter and depicting him as a blazingly fast steed. Their depictions often focus more on his speed and power, as well as his flowing mane.

"Thump once for what was lost. Thump twice for what is here. Thump thrice for what is coming." - Common Carrovian morning prayer

The Still Pool
Lady of the Quiet Water
▾

Revered as a peaceful otter floating in an ancient, reflective lake, the Still Pool is the goddess of peace, sanctuary, and hidden places. Her shrines are built beside ponds and slow rivers, never on moving water. She is the patron deity of Whiskerburrow, and her clergy will tell you that their philosophy of community and careful listening reflects her nature perfectly. To harm someone who has claimed sanctuary at one of her shrines is considered a grave sin.

Whiskerburrow scholars debate endlessly whether the Still Pool predates the Great Awakening, or whether the Awakening itself was her doing, a gift of quiet understanding poured into every creature.

The Great Sow
Mother of Plenty
▾

Carved into the every grain silo in Iron-Tusk and Whiskerburrow, the Great Sow is depicted as a large boar whose belly is always full and whose hooves bless every field. The Love Bird
Protector of Beauty and Passion
▾

Depicted as two swans interlocked, their necks forming the shape of a heart. The Love Bird is a goddess of beauty in all its forms. Be they sights, sounds, smells, tastes, or feelings, the experience of pleasure is her domain. The feeling of love at first sight is said to be a direct divine intervention of the love bird, caused by an arrow shot by her cherrubic swan archers. Newlyweds of every nation and species give an offering of silks, perfumes, and beautiful trinkets to her temples to bless their marriage.

"Beauty is more than skin deep; it issues from the core of one's being and shows one's fair (or foul) face to the world." - Lovebirdian saying.

The Prismatic Peahen
Goddess of Pride and Party Animals
▾

Depicted as a peafowl of such magnificent color that early temple murals had to be repainted twice because artists kept running out of pigment. Although her colors and plumage outshine even the most resplendant peacock, she is understood to be a peahen. This aspect has made her beloved by queer animalfolk across Everden, and a symbol of pride, acceptance, and understanding. Her clergy, called Party Animals, believe that pride, properly understood, is not a sin at all, but a form of worship. They're well-loved most everywhere for their love of bright, colorful parties and parades, held to celebrate even the most humdrum of occasions.

"Wait, it's the one month anniversary of your 25th birthday? Why didn't you say so sooner? Sounds like it's time to PAAAAARTY!" - Something you're likely to hear if you're friends with a Party Animal

The Ink-Nosed One
Keeper of the Written Word
▾

A small, perpetually distracted mouse with an ink-stained snout and spectacles made from smoothed glass. The Ink-Nosed One is the patron of scribes, cartographers, and anyone whose life depends on the written word. The Whiskerburrow Archive considers itself his temple, and their Archivists are his clergy. He is said to have hidden pieces of the Smooth One's history in plain sight, waiting for someone clever enough to read them.

The Golden Collars
Patron of the Worthy Deal
▾

Depicted as a regal two-headed creature with one cat head and one dog head. She is the goddess of honest commerce, fair trade, and the social contract. Her temples look much like banks, because they are banks. Merchants swear their contracts before her priests, and debts acknowledged under her seal are considered sacred obligations. The Dual Monarchs of Petsburg are her stewards, though the Church takes pains to remain above politics. The nation of Goldcollar takes it's name from her.

She makes no distinction between the wealthy merchant and the market mouse, only between those who honor their deals and those who do not.

Cunning Reynard
The Fox Who Walks Between
▾

Although some in Everden malign him as a god of thieves, he is more a god of the space between truth and lie, of cleverness and trickery. Reynard is a lean red fox in a green cloak, always smiling, always up to something. His worship is officially tolerated in Goldcollar, where lawyers quietly maintain a small shrine in most courthouses, and openly celebrated in Moonhowl, where cunning is survival. He has no formal temples, only back rooms, whisper-networks, and the smile shared between people who both know they are being clever at each other.

Puss-in-Boots
The Cat Who Made His Own Luck
▾

Where Reynard works in shadows and half-truths, Puss-in-Boots operates entirely in the open, with complete audacity. He is depicted as a swaggering cat in tall boots and a wide-brimmed hat, rapier sword at his hip, and he is celebrated for his dashing mortal deeds before ascending to godhood. He holds domain over good fortune, skill, victory, and adventurers. He is the patron of the ambitious poor, the self-made, and anyone who has ever refused to accept the status quo. His followers believe that confidence and conviction are their own form of magic. Devotees have no interest in lies. Their texts say he prefers to tell people exactly what he intends and then do it before they have a chance to tell him no.

"He never pretended to be something he wasn't. He simply insisted on becoming it." - Pusserian saying

The Forge Boar
He Who Does Not Yield
▾

Often depicted as a massive boar with a scarred eyes and a broken tusk reforged in black iron, giving the nation of Iron-Tusk it's name. He is the god of honorable warfare, the forge, and the will to endure. His clergy are called Tuskers, warrior priests who wield hammers and blades. They consider retreat a theological question requiring intense deliberation. He respects strength and despises cowardice, but has no love for cruelty. He is said to have given the first horse their iron shoes, enabling the great cavalry charges that made other nations respect the military might of Boar and Horsefolk.

Shakiri
First Hunter, Last Witness
▾

A beautiful she-wolf who moves with a deliberate grace. Shakiri is the patron of hunters, rangers, and those who venture into the wild places where the Silent still roam in great and dangerous numbers. Her teachings are simple: take only what is needed, honor the kill, and waste nothing. Moonhowl's young hunters dedicate their first successful Silent hunt to her. She also governs the difficult question of what separates the Awakened from the Silent, a topic her clergy address with nuance.

The Blind Hound
He Who Judges
▾

Depicted as a long haired white dog with a blindfold across his eyes and one paw missing, the Blind Hound is the god of justice. The missing paw is not a wound his followers mourn, it's believed he gave it willingly to seal away something dangerous, and he would do it again. His devotees are judges, city watchfolk, and paladins, who must parse the separation between morality and law, the difference between the letter of the law and the spirit. He is not a gentle god, and his justice upon the wicked can be brutal. Worshipers of The Blind Hound are often looked at with distrust or scorn by those with more nuanced views on ethics and justice.

"A good dog is a hammer in the hand of The Blind One" - Common criticism of the Hound's followers.

The Blinding Crest
Lord of the Unveiled Sky
▾

Whereas the Dawnthumper is the sun as hope and beginning, the Blinding Crest is the sun as law and judgment. He is depicted as a massive frilled lizard with scales of glimmering gold and eyes too bright to look at directly. The people of Sunreach believe the Smooth Ones vanished because they built their towers so tall they blocked his light. His temples are open to the sky, and his rites are performed at high noon. His clergy can be intense company.

Theologians in Whiskerburrow believe the Dawnthumper and the Blinding Crest are the same divine entity, simply perceived differently across cultures. The clergy of both gods find this suggestion blasphemous.

The Lunar Queen
She Who Lights the Night
▾

Depicted as a luminous white moth drifting across a night sky, trailing moon-dust from her wings. She is the goddess of the moon, navigation, and the comfort of darkness. It is said that her moon-dust created the stars in the sky, and sailors use those stars to navigate, and night-shift workers whisper thanks when the moon is full. She has a devoted following among wolf-kin and dogfolk, who howl insticively at the full moon as a form of worship.

She and the Night Queen are considered theological opposites, and devotees of the Lunar Queen have an intense dislike of the Night Queen.

The Patient Badger
He Who Waits at the End of Every Tunnel
▾

An enormous, still badger seated in a chamber of black stone, lantern in one great paw and scales in the other. He is not feared, merely respected, much as one respects the coming of winter. The Patient Badger judges the dead fairly and without emotion. His clergy are the undertakers, gravediggers, and hospice keepers of Everden, and among the most unfairly disliked clerics in any city. He has jurisdiction over the undead, which he considers an abomination against the natural order, and he acts on that opinion with some force.

The Night Queen
Lady of Silken Ruin
▾

A black cat dressed in violet silk, half-hidden in shadow. The Night Queen is the goddess of secrets kept too long, grief that has rotted into turmoil, and the comfort of despair. She is not entirely evil; her clergy provide genuine solace to those in mourning, and she claims domain over secrets hidden by the darkness. However, her darkest teachings concern loss, and the idea that what is gone is in some ways more real than what remains. Her faith has grown more popular in Carrovia in recent years.

She and the Lunar Queen are considered theological opposites, though scholars note they serve some of the same mourners. Devotees despise followers of the Lunar Queen.

Languages of Everden

Everden has it's own set of languages. Use these instead of the PHB language list during character creation. Your character knows Collartongue and ONE other language from this list. Your class and other features may give you additional languages, such as Druidic or Thieves' Cant.

Collartongue
Script: Repurposed Smooth One lettering - Common to all

The common trade tongue of Goldcollar and most of the civilized world. It evolved from several older languages over centuries of commerce. Every character speaks this by default. If someone in Everden speaks one language, it is this one.

Treat Collartongue like Common during character creation.
Whispersqueak
Script: Flowing cursive

The native tongue of Carrovia and Whiskerburrow, spoken by rabbits, mice, and most small prey-folk. Used by academics, poets, and keepers of Great Awakening lore. It carries more vocabulary for concepts of memory, loss, and community than any other language in Everden.

Ironmouth
Script: Runic

The language of boars, horses, and the industrial folk of Iron-Tusk. Clipped, efficient, and loud. Foreigners often describe it as sounding permanently impatient. Its runic script was designed to be carved into metal, and most Ironmouth signage still is.

Scalese
Script: Pictographic

Spoken by lizards, toads, and the ancient peoples of Sunreach. One of the oldest living languages in Everden, with roots that may predate the Awakening itself. Its pictographic script is found on ruin walls throughout the jungle, some of it predating anything scholars can reliably date.

Howltongue
Script: None (oral tradition only)

The language of wolves, foxes, and the nomadic peoples of Moonhowl. Heavily tonal, making it notoriously difficult for outsiders to learn. A wrong pitch can change a greeting into an insult. Most Moonhowl folk use collartongue for writing, but only if strictly necessary.

Plumesong
Script: Musical Notation

Spoken by birds of all kinds. Contains sounds that no non-avian throat can fully reproduce, meaning most non-speakers learn only a rough approximation. Native speakers can always tell when they are being spoken to by an outsider, and they find it charming in the way that is almost condescending.

Smoothtalk
Script: Classical Roman-style lettering

A dead language. The writing of the Smooth Ones, found today only in ruin inscriptions and crumbling ancient texts. Nobody speaks it natively or knows for certain what it actually sounded like. Scholars reconstruct it's pronunciation from best guesses and argue about it constantly.

Requires 14 Intelligence or higher to learn.
